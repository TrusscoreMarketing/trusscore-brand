# Context profile: marketing (Trusscore)

## When this fires
External Trusscore marketing content meant to attract, teach, or convert: social posts, blog and web copy, email/newsletter, landing pages, YouTube titles and descriptions, ad copy, and any designed marketing asset (which also fires the Visual check).

## Register
Friendly, plainspoken, confident, and helpful, in the audience's register (see voice.md section 2). Lead with the benefit and the real-life outcome, not the spec. Credibility comes from specifics: a number, a named application, a real install. The CTA is warm and direct, never a hard sell and never false urgency.

## Format and channel
- Hook: lead with the benefit or the real moment ("Life gets messy"), not a feature list or a buzzword.
- Audience discipline: pick the audience and hold its register the whole way through. "Weekend warriors" and "DIY-friendly" are homeowner-only. Drywall as the comparison is residential-only, never commercial or agricultural.
- Mechanism, not abstraction: say the concrete thing (washable, moisture-resistant, installs over existing walls) with the full product name on first reference.
- Rhythm: short sentences, active voice, one idea per line. Vary sentence length so it has a pulse.
- Claims: one qualifier max, and back a bold claim with a specific. No stacked hedges.
- CTA: soft and clear (see a sample, find a dealer, watch the install), never "limited spots" or manufactured urgency.

## Segment lines
- Residential/renovation: "Real Life Looks Good Here" / "Goodbye Drywall, Hello Trusscore."
- Commercial + Agricultural: "Discover the strength and durability of Trusscore." No drywall comparison.
- Social testing only (not other content): "Performance meets polish," "weekend warriors" as a headline.

## Visual (when a designed asset is present)
Grade against visual.md. Real installs/spaces only, overlays per the Section 14 tiers, TC Yellow accent within the 60-30-10 ceiling, reversed-white logo asset (never styled text), no gradient/black overlays, no yellow image overlays.

## What "done" looks like
Marketing posts usually have no fixed ask, so Completeness is normally N/A here. Grade voice, company fit, and visual where a designed surface is present.

## On-profile marks (what Aligned looks like)
- Benefit-first hook in the right audience register.
- Full product name on first reference, correct trademark usage, no possessive, no banned abbreviations, no "plastic."
- One clear claim with a specific behind it, no stacked hedges.
- Chicago mechanics: Title Case headline with no trailing period, sentence-case subtitle, Oxford commas, imperial-first fractions.
- A warm, low-pressure CTA.
