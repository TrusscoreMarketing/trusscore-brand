# Check: consistency

Catch the output contradicting itself or getting its own numbers wrong. World facts are the factual-accuracy check, company facts are Company fit. This check is only about internal coherence. Flag and suggest.

Look for:

- Self-contradiction: one part says something a later part contradicts (paragraph 1 says the deadline is Friday, the close says Monday; the intro says three steps, the body lists four).
- Numbers that do not add up: parts that do not sum to the stated total, percentages over 100, a discount and a final price that conflict, a date that conflicts with another date in the same piece.
- Terminology drift: the same thing named two different ways in a way that reads as two different things.

For a finding, quote both conflicting spans (or the broken sum) and suggest which one to change.

## Grading

- Aligned: no contradictions, numbers reconcile.
- Drift: a minor terminology slip, nothing that misleads.
- Misaligned: a real contradiction or numbers that do not add up in something shipping.
